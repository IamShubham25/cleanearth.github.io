Types of Waste

Understanding different types of waste is the first step toward proper waste management and environmental protection.

Biodegradable Waste

Organic waste that can be broken down by microorganisms into simpler molecules. Examples include food scraps, yard waste, and paper products.

Proper Disposal: Composting is the ideal method, turning waste into nutrient-rich soil.

Non-Biodegradable Waste

Materials that cannot be broken down by natural processes. Examples include plastics, metals, and glass.

Proper Disposal: Recycling is crucial. Many non-biodegradable materials can be repurposed indefinitely.

Hazardous Waste

Waste that poses substantial threats to public health or the environment. Examples include batteries, chemicals, and medical waste.

Proper Disposal: Special handling required. Never dispose with regular trash. Use designated collection facilities.

Electronic Waste: The Growing Crisis

E-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream in the world. Discarded electronics contain toxic materials like lead and mercury that can leach into soil and water. Many valuable materials in electronics can be recovered through proper recycling...

Always recycle electronics through certified e-waste recyclers rather than throwing them in the trash.

The Benefits of Proper Organic Waste Management

When biodegradable waste ends up in landfills, it decomposes anaerobically, producing methane - a potent greenhouse gas. By composting organic waste instead, we can create nutrient-rich soil amendments while reducing greenhouse gas emissions...

Home composting is simple to start and can divert up to 30% of household waste from landfills.
